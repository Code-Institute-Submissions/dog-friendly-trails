WHY LEAVE POOCH AT HOME?

The quantity of dog friendly trails are getting reduced all of the time.

Summer 2022 saw dogs banned from Blue Flag beaches for example.

Enjoy Good Company

The saying "a dog is man's best friend" rings through for all dog owners. Taking your dogs on hikes enhances the bond you have with your dog. Dogs really love spending time with their owners.The added dimension of having your dog on a hike seems to energise and motivate when you hit the steep slope that is testing your endurance. Better than any motivational fitness coach.

Avoid Kennel Fees

If you need to travel away to take on the hike, like for a weekend or something, then getting your dog minded is problematic and often expensive. So much more convenient to bring your dog along. Dog friendly accommodation usually charge a very nominal fee when booking your dog in as an additional guest.

Keep Your Dog Happy

Leaving your dogs behind when you go for a hike is very stressful for your dog. Have you ever seen a dog who likes being left behind. They always seem to know you are going for a walk or a hike. Taking your dog with you on hikes is great for them to experience new smells and surroundings. Of course they are also great company and enhance any hike, their excitement is quite infectious.

Keep Your Dog Healthy

Keeping your dog in good physical shape keeps painful ailments away and even prolongs their lifespan. It cuts down on visits to the Vet as well. Hikes are particularly good for exercising your dog, the variety of terrain gives them a much better workout than a walk on the footpath or road.

This site is dedicated to providing all dog lovers with information on dog friendly trails. Our subscribers help keep the site up to date with all the latest changes in dog related rules and regulations on Irelands walking trails.
